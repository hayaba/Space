"SHOOTING STARS"

It isn't really a star

Asteroids are rocky objects orbiting the Sun – with millions of them contained in solar orbit in the asteroid belt situated between Mars and Jupiter. Those of size less than 300 km have irregular shape because their gravity is too weak to compress them into spheres. Some of the asteroids, such as Ceres with a diameter of about 10 6 m, are large enough to be considered as “minor planets”.
